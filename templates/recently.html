Research Updated: Dec 2025

Skin Safe Electrodes – enabling advancements for medical and wearable electronics.

Using light allows electronics to fit to the skin naturally rather than sitting on top of it. These devices are able to measure electrical signals from the body indicating muscle activity for example.

This is incredibly important as traditional electrodes can be uncomfortable or even inaccurate if they are to move. Direct printing to the skin gives better contact and improves signal quality; whilst offering an avenue towards medical monitoring becoming simpler and less invasive.

The study was conducted by researchers at Linkoping University and Lund University in Sweden. Their findings concluded that visible light can be used to form electrodes made from conductive plastics without relying on hazardous chemicals.
