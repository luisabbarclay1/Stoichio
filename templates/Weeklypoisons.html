What to Avoid

Everyday chemistry, explained simply. This page highlights common substances people try to reduce exposure to, plus what words to look for on labels.

PFAS (“Forever chemicals”) in non-stick cookware

PFAS are a family of chemicals sometimes associated with non-stick coatings and “stain/water-resistant” treatments. Many people choose to avoid them due to long persistence in the environment and ongoing health research.

Look for:

  • “PFA-free” claims (can be helpful, but still verify)
  • Coating terms like PTFE / Teflon®
  • Older terminology: PFOA / PFOS (legacy PFAS)

Phthalates & BPA in plastics + cosmetics packaging

Phthalates and BPA are commonly discussed because they can be found in some plastics and packaging, and may be linked to hormone-related concerns in research. People often try to reduce them as a precaution.

Look for:

  • Phthalates: DEP, DBP, DEHP, DINP, DIDP (or “phthalate”)
  • BPA (and sometimes BPS, BPF as “replacements”)
  • Plastic codes: #3 (PVC) and #7 (“other”) are commonly flagged
  • “Fragrance / Parfum” (can sometimes hide phthalates)

Parabens in personal care products

Parabens are preservatives used to stop bacteria and mould growth in cosmetics. Some people avoid them due to hormone-related concerns, even though they’re effective preservatives and heavily debated.

Look for:

  • Methylparaben
  • Ethylparaben
  • Propylparaben
  • Butylparaben

More info: PFAS & cookware
  • What PFAS means: a large group of persistent chemicals (not just one substance).
  • Where it shows up: non-stick coatings (often PTFE-related) and some “water/stain resistant” items.
  • What to do (simple): if you’re trying to reduce PFAS, search labels for “PFAS-free” and avoid PTFE/Teflon® coatings.

Keywords to search: PFAS, PTFE, Teflon, PFOA, PFOS.

More info: Phthalates & BPA
  • Phthalates: sometimes used in fragrances and to make plastics flexible.
  • BPA: historically used in some plastics and resins; “BPA-free” products may use alternatives like BPS/BPF.
  • What to do (simple): check ingredients for fragrance/parfum and common phthalate abbreviations; prefer clearly labelled packaging.

Keywords to search: DEP, DBP, DEHP, DINP, BPA, BPS, BPF, fragrance, parfum.

More info: Parabens
  • Why they’re used: preservatives keep products from spoiling.
  • Why some avoid them: debate around potential hormone-related effects; many products advertise “paraben-free”.
  • What to do (simple): scan the ingredient list for anything ending in -paraben.

Keywords to search: methylparaben, ethylparaben, propylparaben, butylparaben, paraben.

Note: This site is educational. Research and regulations change, and “avoid” lists depend on personal preferences, sensitivities, and risk tolerance.
